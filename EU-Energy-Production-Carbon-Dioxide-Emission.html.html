Analyzing the correlation between Energy Production and Carbon Dioxide Emission in the European Union

Introduction

Since the industrial revolution, the quality of the air we breathe has deteriorated considerably - mainly as a result of human activities. The burning of fossil fuels and biomass, as well as the dramatic rise in traffic on our roads all, contribute to air pollution which leads to serious problems for both health and the environment. Particulate matter, nitrogen dioxide, and ground-level ozone are recognized as the three pollutants that most significantly affect human health.

Since early 1970s, the European Union has been working to improve air quality by controlling emissions of harmful substances into the atmosphere, improving fuel quantity, and by integrating environmental requirements into the transport and energy sectors. In 2013, European Commission adopted a Clean Air Policy Package setting objective for 2020 and 2030 to tackle air pollutants and improve air quality. In 2018, Commission adopted a Communication “A Europe that protects: Clean air for all” that provide national, regional and local actors practical help to improve air quality in Europe.

Factors causing Carbon Dioxide emission level to increase

From the Graph below we notice that Fuel Combusion Activities lead to most carbon dioxide emission.

Further, we drill down into Fuel Combustion Activities to determine what kind of Fuel Combustion activities lead to high carbon dioxide emission


Fuel Combustion Activities and their share into Carbon Dioxide Emission (Tonne), 2016

Energy Industries, Transport and Manufacturing Industries and Construction are the major contributors within Fuel Combustion Activities that lead to higher carbon dioxide emission. We further drill down into these categories to identify major contributors.


  • Energy Industries - Public Electricity and Heat Production is the major contributor

  • Transport - Road Transport is the major contributor

    • Transport and their share into Fuel Combustion Activities (Tonne), 2016


  • Manufacturing Industries and Construction - Iron and Steel, Non-metallic minerals and Other Manufacturing Industries and Construction are the major contributor

    • Manufacturing Industries and Construction and their share into Fuel Combustion Activities (Tonne), 2016

    Summary

  • Nuclear energy, Renewable (Wind, Hydro and Solar), Solid Fuels and Natural Gases are the major sources of Energy Production in European Union
  • There is a positive correlation between Energy Production and Carbon Dioxide Emissions
  • Fuel Combusion Activities lead to most carbon dioxide emission
  • Energy Industries, Transport and Manufacturing Industries and Construction are the major contributors within Fuel Combustion Activities that lead to higher carbon dioxide emission
  • Public Electricity and Heat Production is major contributor within Energy Industries, Road Transport is the major contributor within Transport and Iron and Steel, Non-metallic minerals and Other Manufacturing Industries and Construction are the major contributor that lead to higher Fuel Combusion Activities
